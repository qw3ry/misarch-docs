## Design Choices in the Domain Model

In the following, an `Entity` of the domain model is formatted verbatim, and a _Bounded Context_ is formatted italic.

### How the _Inventory_ handles *Return*s

The domain model does not automatically add returned items back to the _Inventory_. They need to be added manually, similar to a shipment from the manufacturer, or thrown away.

### How `ShoppingCart` work

- A `User` cannot reserve items by putting them in the `ShoppingCart`: The _Inventory_ contains `ProductItem`s as instances of a `ProductVariant`. These `ProductItem`s do not know about existing `ShoppingCart`s or `ShoppingCartItem`s. Thus, when the user wants to _finish_ the order, the system must check whether the `ProductItem` is still in stock, since it could have been ordered by another user in the meantime.
- A `User` can have exactly one `ShoppingCart` at a time, independent of the client device used.

### What `Invoice`s and `Payment`s mean

- An `Invoice` is associated with exactly one `Order`. Split invoices for parts of the order are not supported.
- A `Payment` is only created after the associated `Invoice` has been paid.
- The store supports only payment methods where the payment is completed before the shipping of any ordered items.

### How `Discount` work

- A `Coupon` is a promotional offer. It allows a `User` to apply a `Discount` to a `Product`. Each `Coupon` is associated with a specific `Discount`.
- The store only offers `Discount`s in percentages. The store does not have gift cards.
- The `Discount` can have whitelist filters by `Product`, `ProductVariant`, and `Category`. If filters are present, the `Discount` can be applied to `Product`s matching any filter. If no filters are present, the `Discount` can be applied to all products.

### International shops and business accounts

- Each `ProductVariantVersion` has its `TaxRate` specified. Thus, the store cannot easily operate in multiple tax rate regions.
- Tax deduction for business accounts are not supported by the system, since the `TaxRate` is fixed for all `User`s.

### `Notification`s

- A `Notification` is within-the-system; they are not forwarded to emails, text messages, or anything else.
- `Notification`s are read-only. `User`s can only receive notifications, they cannot reply to them.
- Each `Notification` is sent to exactly one user. There are no broadcasts.

### How `User`s relate to their `Account`, `Wishlist`s, and `Address`es

- To be able to order something from the store, a `User` needs an `Account`. This implies that the store does not support something like "checkout as guest".
- Addresses stored by the system do not have to be unique physical addresses. The system may store multiple instances of the same physical address. One `Address` cannot be related to multiple `User` instances.
- A user's `Wishlist`s are private and cannot be seen by other users.

### Regarding _Category_

The store has no `Category` hierarchy.
